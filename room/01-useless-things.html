Useless Things Are Not Always Useless

small repair no audience sincere object exists anyway

Some things are useless only to systems that do not know how to measure relief.

I make useless things.

At least, that is what they probably look like from the outside.

  • Music or sounds without listeners.
  • Visual experiments with no client, no brief, no purpose, no promise.
  • Tiny effects that almost nobody will notice.
  • Private creations that exist only because something inside me wanted them to exist.
  • Small websites nobody asked for.
  • Hidden pages that may never be found.

There may be no audience.

Sometimes I am the only person who will ever see them.

And still, I make them.

Not because I think they are important.

Not because I am trying to prove a point.

Not because I want to rebel against productivity in some clean, theoretical way.

Most of the time, it is simpler than that.

My brain starts building.

It takes a strange idea, an image, a texture, a page, a sound, a small visual accident, and it begins arranging things until the inside of my head becomes slightly less unbearable.

That is not a business model.

That is not a strategy.

That is barely even a decision.

It is more like a reflex.

A small private repair.

The problem is that the world does not always know what to do with things that do not explain themselves.

It asks: What is it for? Can it be used? Can it be sold? Can it help your career? Can it become a project? Can it become a skill? Can it become a line on a CV? Can it justify the time you gave to it?

And sometimes the honest answer is no.

No, it will not make money.

No, it will not advance my life in any measurable direction.

No, it will not become useful to society in a way that sounds good in a meeting.

No, I cannot always explain why it had to exist.

It exists because making it made me feel something.

Sometimes that is enough.

Or at least, I need it to be enough.

Because when everything has to be useful, I start feeling useless by extension.

If the things I love are treated as pointless, if the things that make me breathe are treated as childish, decorative, absurd or unnecessary, then something in me hears the same sentence applied to myself.

That is where the sadness enters.

Not loudly.

Not dramatically.

It just sits there.

The quiet idea that maybe I should be producing something clearer.

Something more serious.

Something more profitable.

Something easier to defend.

Something with a function.

But I do not want every part of my life to become a function.

I do not want every gesture to become a performance.

I do not want every creation to become an argument.

I do not want every hour to stand in front of a judge and prove that it was not wasted.

Some things are allowed to be small.

Some things are allowed to be strange.

Some things are allowed to be useless in the eyes of the world and still be necessary to the person who made them.

A hidden page can be a refuge.

A private image can be a room.

A tiny animation can be a pulse.

A bit of music can be an expressed emotion.

A strange website can become a place where the soul quietly repairs one loose wire.

I like the idea that someone may find one of these things one day.

Not everyone.

Not an audience.

Not a crowd.

Just someone.

Someone curious enough to click too far.

Someone who opens a page that was not placed in the main corridor.

Someone who notices the useless detail and understands that it was not useless to me.

Maybe they will tell me.

Maybe they will not.

The possibility is enough to make the room feel less empty.

I do not need these things to convince anyone.

That may be why I love them.

They do not ask me to become better.

They do not ask me to be efficient.

They do not ask me to justify why I kept them, made them, repaired them, arranged them or refused to throw them away.

They simply exist.

And sometimes existence without demand feels like mercy.

I have emotional attachments to objects, to fragments, to creations that would probably look replaceable to someone else.

But they are not replaceable to me.

A thing is not only its function.

A thing is also the moment it entered your life.

The attention you gave it.

The feeling it carried.

The private reason nobody else can fully access.

Some objects survive.

Some ideas survive.

Some ridiculous little projects survive.

Some pages survive even if nobody visits them.

And when something survives long enough to become part of your inner landscape, throwing it away is not neutral.

It is not always cleaning.

Sometimes it feels like erasing a witness.

Maybe that is why I keep building and keeping useless things.

They witness parts of me that the useful world does not know how to read.

They let me exist outside the roles.

Not student.

Not worker.

Not developer.

Not useful person.

Not functional object.

Just someone making something because making it felt good to the soul.

That sentence sounds fragile.

Maybe it is.

But I think fragile things deserve space too.

I would rather make something small and sincere than something large and empty.

I would rather lose time inside a useless creation that came from the heart than save time for a life that has no room for softness.

Useless things are not always useful.

That is not the point.

They do not need to become useful to deserve protection.

They do not need to become productive to deserve time.

They do not need to become understandable to deserve existence.

Some things do not save you by solving anything.

They save you by giving you a place to put a feeling.

A shape.

A room.

A surface.

A tiny useless world where nothing asks you to become profitable before allowing you to breathe.

Maybe that is enough.

Not because useless things secretly become useful.

Because usefulness was never the highest form of existence.
