Maintenance Ritual

I build tools because friction annoys me.

Not the dramatic kind of friction. The small kind. The kind that hides inside a form, a file format, a missing button, a bad interface, a repetitive calculation, a workstation that behaves like it resents the person using it.

These things look small from the outside. They are not always small when you live inside them every day.

A badly designed interface can quietly exhaust people. A repetitive task can become a tiny prison with a progress bar. A hidden piece of information can turn a simple day into a municipal procedure.

When I can remove one of those obstacles, I usually do.

I do not have much money. I am not in a position where generosity can be spectacular. But I can give time. I can give code. I can give small repairs.

A tool does not have to be glorious. It just has to make someone’s day less hostile.

Care is sometimes only a smaller obstacle.

Less pressure is still a repair.

Every repeated task leaves a mark.

A tool can be quiet and still matter.

No useful repair needs applause.

Boris
