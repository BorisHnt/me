About the Operator

My name is Boris Hanicotte.

I used to work in medical technical support. Now I study software development at 42 Marseille, where I negotiate with pointers, terminals, broken layouts and my own execution stack.

Previous Life

Before moving deeper into software, I worked around medical and technical environments: support requests, practical systems, users who needed answers, machines that preferred silence, and problems that had to be solved without theater.

That background left a useful residue. I like tools that explain themselves, interfaces that do not pretend to be smarter than the operator, and systems that remain calm when something breaks. inspection pending

Current System

I now study software development at 42 Marseille. The method is direct: read, build, break, inspect, repair, repeat until the machine stops making a specific complaint.

I write web interfaces, small utilities, Python scripts, school support tools and experiments that begin as simple files and quietly become small machines. operator mismatch
